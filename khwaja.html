Khwaja Gharib Nawaz

The Life, Teachings, and Legacy of Hazrat Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti (R.A.)

Life and Spiritual Journey

Hazrat Khwaja Moinuddin Hasan Chishti, affectionately known as Khwaja Gharib Nawaz (Benefactor of the Poor), was born in 1141 CE in Sistan (now in Iran). He descended from the lineage of the Holy Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) through both his parents. His early life was marked by piety, generosity, and a deep sense of compassion for the poor and needy.

After extensive travels in search of spiritual enlightenment, Khwaja Gharib Nawaz arrived in Ajmer, India, where he dedicated his life to spreading the message of love, peace, and unity. His teachings emphasized the importance of serving humanity, regardless of religion, caste, or social status.

Teachings and Philosophy

  • Love for all and malice towards none.
  • Service to humanity as the highest form of worship.
  • Promotion of interfaith harmony and tolerance.
  • Detachment from material possessions.

His Khanqah (spiritual center) became a sanctuary for people of all walks of life, where they could receive guidance, food, and shelter.

Annual Urs Celebration

The Urs of Khwaja Gharib Nawaz is held annually in Ajmer, attracting millions of devotees from around the world. It marks his union with the Divine and is celebrated with Qawwali (Sufi devotional music), prayers, and the distribution of Langar (free meals).

This sacred event is a powerful reminder of his eternal message of love, compassion, and selfless service.
