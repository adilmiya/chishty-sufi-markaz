The Life and Teachings of Hazrat Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti (H.A.)

Life of Khwaja Moinuddin Chishti

Hazrat Khwaja Moinuddin Hasan Chishti (H.A.), revered as Khwaja Gharib Nawaz, was one of the most prominent Sufi saints of the Indian subcontinent. Born in Sistan (present-day Iran) in 1141 CE, he traveled extensively in pursuit of spiritual knowledge and enlightenment before settling in Ajmer, India.

Through his compassion, humility, and profound spirituality, Khwaja Gharib Nawaz spread the message of universal love, peace, and service to humanity. His teachings transcended barriers of religion, caste, and creed, attracting followers from all walks of life.

Teachings

  • Love towards all and malice towards none
  • Service to humanity as the highest form of devotion
  • Equality and compassion for all, regardless of background
  • Detachment from material desires and focus on spiritual growth

Annual Urs Celebration

The Urs of Khwaja Gharib Nawaz is celebrated annually in Ajmer, marking his union with the Divine. Devotees from around the world gather at the Dargah Sharif to offer prayers, seek blessings, and participate in qawwalis, langar, and other spiritual activities.
